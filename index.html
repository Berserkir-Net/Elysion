Norman Maclean

After teaching in secondary schools for most of my life, I now find myself in the happy situation where it is possible to pursue other fields, most of which have been areas of long-term interest.  But nibbling at the edges… giving little time previously to these, has always been an irritant.

I am interested in now making myself available in the following spheres, all of which have provided me with plenty of experience, despite the occasional nature of my involvement up to this point:

 

PUBLIC SPEAKING: 

I have wide experience in this having conducted numerous lectures and seminars here in Gisborne and elsewhere in the North Island. These include:

Guest speaking at Rotary dinners; lectures for the Waikato University Continuing Education programme; talks to University of the Third Age and similar groups; broadcasting slots for National Radio (Morning Comment and Sunday Supplement programmes;) addresses to the Sea of Faith conference and associated organisations.

THEATRICAL PRODUCTIONS:

With eighteen productions as director for Gisborne Unity Theatre and three for Gisborne Theatre Arts, plus major shows for Gisborne Boys’ High School where I taught drama over a period of nine years, I am able to offer my services as a director.  I am able to provide with this considerable experience in stage design and costume design: most of the sets for my productions have been designed and painted personally.

I am prepared to consider employment in these fields for organisations outside of this region, subject to discussion of terms and the signing of an appropriate contract.

VISUAL ARTS:

With many years practice as Head of Art in both Gisborne Boys’ and Lytton High schools, I am able to offer tuition in the fields of drawing, painting and print-making which are all particular interests.  I have had major exhibitions of paintings and prints  - mostly in zinc-plate and solar plate media – and have works held in collections throughout New Zealand and overseas.

Illustration in the form of coloured posters for teachers of Classics are printed by Polygraphia in Auckland: I have produced a wide range of these being commissioned to design them for the company many years ago.  I have always leaned towards imaginative drawing and in the 1980’s, I had a series of cartoons published by the New Zealand Listener.  Caricatures and humorous illustration have become major interests.

I was a founder member of the Gisborne Printmakers’ group that began in 1976 and am currently co-ordinator of the Gisborne Artists’ Society committee.  I also run the Life Class for this society: drawing from the nude model.

Associated with this is a strong fascination with art history which I taught for many years.  Particular enthusiasms are the use of iconography and symbolism generally; classical Greek and Roman art and architecture; Christian symbolism; the Italian High Renaissance and the moves to modernism that occurred in Europe during the late 19th-early 20th century.

MARRIAGE & FUNERAL CELEBRANT:

Since 1996 I have been a registered marriage celebrant and have conducted over one hundred ceremonies both locally and elsewhere in the country, usually in outdoor settings selected by the couple to be wed.  I am able to offer personalized and original formats for this, suiting the wording and conduct of the ceremony to the wishes of each couple.

Over the last decade, I have increasingly been called upon to also conduct funerals and have now experienced the whole gamut of these from the farewell appropriate to someone at the end of a long life to the much more sensitive handling of sudden and tragic death.

Marriages and funerals are rites of passage which mean a great deal to me as I have become very aware of society’s lack of ritual and ceremony.

OVERSEAS TOURS:

I have now conducted three of these, leading groups to places of particular interest in the Mediterranean/ Aegean region.  Countries visited include Egypt, Jordan, Israel, Turkey, Greece and Italy.  The focus has always been on classical antiquity; on art and architecture; myth, legend and religious belief and the cultures of each country.

Return visits to places such as Cairo, Petra, Jerusalem, Athens, Rome and Istanbul would be a pleasure should people be interested in finding others to join them on excursions of three to four weeks with full organization of accommodation, transport and tours guaranteed. The trips take in key sites but also focus on the less well known but fascinating places made known through archeology, religion and the great events of antiquity.

WRITING:

As a keen writer, I have had published numerous articles in magazines; short stories broadcast on National Radio and in 2011, a book published in Britain with Christian origins as its subject matter.  Jesus on our own Ground as it is entitled won first place in the 2010 Aston Wylie literary award for an unpublished manuscript for which the prize was $10,000.

I mention this because an ability to word documents or undertake proof reading is a service I am able to provide.  Creative writing courses in which I have participated, provide me with a range of techniques that can easily be communicated to others.

REVELATION:

This novel by Norman Maclean is available on line or in printed format.  It is available from Barnes and Noble, Smash Words or Amazon under Plaisted Publishing in Rotorua.

The book is historical fiction that takes as its focus the writing of the last book of the New Testament, commonly and erroneously accepted as a catalogue of prophecies for the present age when it is actually a coded statement about the conditions prevailing for Christians at the end of the 1st century C.E.

Historians have long recognized that it alludes to the hopes and fears of the early Christians; cryptically discourses on their beliefs and makes numerous references to the calamitous political, social and religious upheavals that occurred both before and after the destruction of the Jewish state by Rome’s legions in 70 C.E.  This approach has been adopted by the author also.

REVELATION the novel covers a sweep of time from the early years of that century to the late 90’s when the protagonist, one, Kleitos, a Hellenized Jew, is narrating the story of his life.  Central to the theme is a secret that he carries with him and cannot reveal until the end: this revelation throws light on the foundational mystery of Christianity – the apparent resurrection of Jesus.

Not a book for Christian conservatives, it does, however, deal in realities.  The reign of assorted emperors; the chaos of the Jewish rebellion against Rome that culminated in the total destruction of Jerusalem and the end of the temple cult; the aggressive, conflicting theological views of early Christian groups and the Neronian persecution, are background to Kleitos’s career that takes him from Caesarea in Judaea to the Greek island of Patmos where he finds himself compelled to care for a bigoted mystic known as John the Elder. This man’s wandering and highly symbolic letter to the seven Christian communities of the Roman east is what became, Revelation, the Biblical book.
